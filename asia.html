Asia - Financial Overview

Asia, the largest and most populous continent, is home to diverse economies ranging from advanced markets to emerging economies. Here's a detailed look at the financial landscape across key countries in Asia.

China

GDP: $14.3 trillion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 25%.

Population: Approximately 1.4 billion.

Financial Market: China has a rapidly growing financial market with major stock exchanges including the Shanghai Stock Exchange (SSE) and Shenzhen Stock Exchange (SZSE).

India

GDP: $3.4 trillion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates ranging from 25% to 30%.

Population: Approximately 1.4 billion.

Financial Market: India’s financial market includes the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and National Stock Exchange (NSE). It has a growing fintech sector.

Japan

GDP: $4.9 trillion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 30%.

Population: Approximately 125 million.

Financial Market: Japan has a well-established financial market with the Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE) being one of the largest in the world.

South Korea

GDP: $1.8 trillion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates ranging from 10% to 25%.

Population: Approximately 52 million.

Financial Market: South Korea's financial market includes the Korea Exchange (KRX) and has a strong focus on technology and innovation.

Indonesia

GDP: $1.2 trillion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 22%.

Population: Approximately 270 million.

Financial Market: Indonesia’s financial market includes the Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) and is growing rapidly with increasing foreign investment.
