Africa - Financial Overview

Africa is a continent with diverse economies and growth potential. Here’s an overview of key financial aspects across major African countries.

Nigeria

GDP: $450 billion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 30%.

Population: Approximately 210 million.

Financial Market: Nigeria has a growing financial market with the Nigerian Stock Exchange (NSE) as a key player.

South Africa

GDP: $419 billion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 28%.

Population: Approximately 60 million.

Financial Market: South Africa’s financial market is well-developed with the Johannesburg Stock Exchange (JSE) being one of the largest in Africa.

Kenya

GDP: $115 billion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 30%.

Population: Approximately 55 million.

Financial Market: Kenya’s financial market includes the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) with growing fintech innovations.

Egypt

GDP: $405 billion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 22.5%.

Population: Approximately 104 million.

Financial Market: Egypt has a developing financial market with the Egyptian Exchange (EGX) as a key player.

Ghana

GDP: $82 billion (2022)

Tax System: Progressive income tax system with corporate tax rates around 25%.

Population: Approximately 32 million.

Financial Market: Ghana’s financial market includes the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) with a growing investment sector.
